	\note[item]{Insect sensory systems include inertial, visual airflow and wing load sensing.}
	% Insect flow sensing
	\note[item]{Insect flow sensing: For instance, in the silkworm moth, bristles along the wing
	  provide information about wing beat frequency}
	% Insect force sensing
	\note[item]{Insect force sensing: In the blowfly the campaniform sensilla operate as load
	  sensors}
	% Bird flow sensing
	\note[item]{Bird flow sensing: Herbst corpuscles are vibration-sensitive mechano-receptors,
	  evidence suggests that they are used to measure airspeed}
	% Bird force sensing
	\note[item]{Bird force sensing: Muscle spindles act as stretch receptors, allowing birds to
	  determine displacements at the base of the wing}
  % Bat flow sensing
	\note[item]{Bat flow sensing: Hairs are involved in sensorimotor flight control by providing
	  aerodynamic feedback. Evidence suggests hairs act as an array of sensors to monitor flight
		speed and/or airflow conditions that indicate stall}
	% Bat force sensing
	\note[item]{Bat force sensing: Muscle spindles}
